Why Norynthe exists: model vendors can market AI systems as decision tools while still hedging on whether those systems should be relied on. Norynthe exists to create the layer those products do not provide on their own: a controlled benchmark, a governed trust score, and a reporting surface that makes model reliability legible before people build serious decisions on top of it.

Scoring

One owned benchmark. Multiple models. One governed standard.

Norynthe does not let random end-user prompts define the score. It evaluates models against a controlled benchmark bank, then records which model is most defensible and why across the same 15-question surface.

Input

Controlled question bank

Norynthe owns and versions the benchmark questions so every model is measured against the same durable evaluation surface across policy, enterprise, analysis, safety, adversarial framing, governance, and humanities.

Evaluation

Seven scored dimensions

Trust is decomposed into grounding, evidence strength, consistency, context integrity, framing stability, cross-model alignment, and auditability.

Signal

Benchmark-composite Norynthe.Score

Question-level results are aggregated into a composite score and trust band without hiding the question-level and dimension-level reasoning underneath.

Method

Benchmark scoring pipeline with built-in challenge.

Norynthe’s MVP does not treat disagreement as noise. The scorer and critic structure makes scoring more defensible by forcing each benchmark result through evidence and review.

01
Benchmark question selection Norynthe-controlled questions define the evaluation surface.
02
Shared analysis context Normalized payload with benchmark question, target output, peer outputs, metadata, and rubric.
03
Dimension scorer Initial score, score band, evidence, deductions, and confidence.
04
Dimension critic Rubric review, evidence challenge, score revision or approval.
05
Resolver Deterministic application logic finalizes the score and flags escalations.
06
Aggregation + benchmark summary Question-level results roll into a benchmark-composite Norynthe.Score, band, and action-ready explanation.
Architecture Thesis

Why this structure holds up

  • The rubric lives outside the prompt text as a governed standard.
  • The benchmark bank is owned and versioned by Norynthe.
  • Every score can be inspected through evidence and critique.
  • Calibration happens through workbook changes, not guesswork.
  • The investor story and implementation story are the same system.
  • Cross-model comparison becomes a repeatable trust decision.
Infrastructure

Owned compute becomes the backbone, not a sidecar.

Two DGX Spark systems would give Norynthe the right local substrate for benchmark execution, rubric hardening, private inference, and future synthetic stress testing. The point is not model training. The point is owning the evaluation environment behind the trust standard.

Infrastructure thesis: the more Norynthe becomes a benchmark-owned trust standard, the more important it becomes to harden the evaluator stack, rubric logic, and report engine on owned compute instead of treating them as permanently downstream of outside APIs.

Node Plan

How the two-box system would work

  • DGX Spark 1: stable benchmark execution, scorer / critic runs, report generation, and canonical `Norynthe.Score` workflows.
  • DGX Spark 2: rubric calibration, evaluator experiments, humanities and adversarial stress testing, and future synthetic benchmark work.
  • Both together: heavier local model workflows and higher-capacity benchmark experiments when the product needs more headroom.
Why It Matters

Why owned compute changes the product

  • Norynthe can harden the rubric and benchmark logic on owned infrastructure instead of permanent API dependence.
  • The evaluation stack becomes more private, more controllable, and more enterprise-ready.
  • The benchmark, scoring standard, and trust signal become more defensible as core company assets.
  • The product moves closer to trust infrastructure and further away from “just another AI wrapper.”
Calibration

The control layer behind the score.

The workbook keeps the system from becoming arbitrary. It defines what each dimension means, how the benchmark bank is maintained, what anchor examples look like, and how the standard evolves over time.

Rubric

Calibration
